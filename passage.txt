Frogs are fascinating creatures that can be found in ponds, rivers, forests, and even gardens. Although many people think frogs are simple animals, they actually play an important role in the ecosystem. Frogs eat insects such as mosquitoes and flies, helping to control insect populations naturally.

Most frogs begin their lives in water. They hatch from eggs and first become tadpoles, which look very different from adult frogs because they have tails and gills. As they grow older, they slowly develop legs and lungs. This process is called metamorphosis.

Frogs have moist skin, so they must stay near water or damp places to survive. Some frogs are brightly coloured to warn predators that they may be poisonous, while others use camouflage to blend into their surroundings.

Unfortunately, frog populations are decreasing around the world because of pollution, habitat destruction, and climate change. Scientists are concerned about this decline because frogs are important indicators of environmental health.
